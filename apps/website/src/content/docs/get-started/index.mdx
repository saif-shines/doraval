**Make your next context work (skills, plugins & more) for your team, community, or self.**

If you're a senior engineer handing skills to new team members, a company publishing resources to the community, or someone who wants agents (and humans) to succeed on the first attempt instead of after days of debugging, this is for you.

### The problem orchestrators face
- Give 10 new engineers (or agents) a skill. Only 3/10 succeed on the first try. 4/10 take hours. 7/10 take a day. 10/10 take days.
- Publish a skill or plugin? Community members hit the same slow ramp.
- Validate that your resource actually works for Claude, Cursor, Codex, or Copilot?
- Want to use your context window (and your agents') more effectively?

It helps you **left-shift success** by making the context you engineer reliable from the first attempt.

## Why this matters for orchestrators

You don't just write one skill. You orchestrate context across teams, agents, models, and time.

When that context is wrong, success is slow and expensive:

- New engineers (or new agents) waste days instead of shipping in hours.
- Published resources fail for most users on first try.
- You can't confidently say "this works for Claude, Cursor, and Copilot."
- Your team's decisions drift because agents (and humans) don't have the right shared context.

doraval turns "it works on my machine with my agent" into "this reliably works for the people and agents I orchestrate."

## How orchestrators use doraval

| Command | Orchestrator job it accelerates | Speed |
|---------|--------------------------------|-------|
| `validate` | Make sure a skill/plugin/hook/MCP actually works (for the right agents) | Instant |
| `claude new` / `cursor new` etc. | Scaffold resources that succeed on first attempt across agents | Instant |
| `init` | One-time setup so your team + agents start with the right context foundation | Instant |
| `providers` | See exactly which agents understand which keywords | Instant |
| `ui` | Stop re-typing. Capture and reuse decisions in a local dashboard | Instant |
| `journal` | Record team principles with pushback so agents (and humans) stay consistent over time | Instant |
| `skill validate` / `skill drift` | Check individual resources or measure how far they've drifted | Instant |

`validate` is the workhorse for left-shifting success. Point it at a directory or GitHub URL and it tells you what's broken before your new engineers or community members waste time on it.

## What orchestrators do next

- `doraval init`: one-time foundation so your team + agents share context from day one (see [Installation](/get-started/installation/)).
- `doraval update`: stay current.
- `doraval providers` + `--for`: target the right agents.
- `doraval claude new` / `cursor new` etc.: create resources that are correct by construction.
- `dora ui`: daily context management without repetitive typing.
- `doraval journal`: capture decisions with pushback so agents and humans don't contradict the past.

The goal is always the same: make the first attempt the successful one.
